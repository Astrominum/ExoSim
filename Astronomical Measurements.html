Astronomical Measurements

  • Solar Mass (M☉)

    A unit of mass equal to the mass of the Sun, typically used in comparison to stars.

  • Jovian Mass (MJ)

    A unit of mass equal to the mass of Jupiter, typically used in comparison to large planets.

  • Earth Mass (M⨁)

    A unit of mass equal to the mass of Earth, typically used in comparison to small or medium-sized planets.

  • Solar Radii (R☉)

    A unit of distance equal to the radius of the Sun, typically used in comparison to stars.

  • Jovian Radii (R J)

    A unit of distance equal to the radius of Jupiter, typically used in comparison to large planets.

  • Earth Radii (R⨁)

    A unit of distance equal to the radius of the Sun, typically used in comparison to large planets.

  • Lightyear

    A unit of distance equal to how far light travels in a year, typically used to measure extents between objects and places in the Universe.

  • Astronomical Unit (AU)

    A unit of distance equal to distance between Earth and the Sun, typically used to measure extents between planets and their star in exoplanetary systems.

  • Absolute Magnitude

    A unit of luminosity measured in how bright an astronomical object looks from Earth, typically used with stars.

  • Kelvin (K)

    A unit of temperature equal to 273.15 degrees more than Celsius, typically used to measure the heat of stars. Kelvin is directly based off of molecular motion, with 0 Kelvin being an impossible point of no particle movement.
